Why Anthropic.

I am especially drawn to Anthropic because I want to build AI that is both highly capable and worthy of trust. My work in production has made me attentive to guardrails, evaluation, provenance, and failure modes — and I want to deepen those instincts at the place where safety and usefulness are treated as inseparable.

I use Claude every day. Not as a tool I picked from a list, but as the runtime I've built an entire skill architecture on top of — 40+ packaged Claude skills, a published framework (ROSTR) for production multi-agent systems, and a methodology for how LLMs should handle consequential decisions differently from how they handle drafting and interpretation.

What I've built in the last year: a two-channel AI inbound system with $9M+ in attributed pipeline, every dollar traceable to a workflow execution ID. A compliance screening engine where the LLM does only semantic mapping and a deterministic rules engine makes every consequential call. A four-agent post-call pipeline I rebuilt from scratch after diagnosing a broken RAG vector index that was causing 84% of failures. I own the failures. I build the audit trails. I prove the revenue.

The role I'm most drawn to is Forward Deployed Engineer — being in the room with enterprise customers, understanding where the model falls short in production contexts, and building the bridge. My background in enterprise sales at Uber, Upwork, and Asana means I understand both the buyer's problem and the architecture required to solve it. I know what it means to discover the real job behind a stated request, and I know how to communicate technical constraints to non-technical stakeholders without condescension.

I also build independently. My products include TrainLola, Replai, HuddleCo, CreditFixer, Artispreneur, and others — organized through Diamitani Industries and connected by ROSTR, my published framework for translating ambiguous ideas into structured, accountable AI systems.

I want to grow in an environment where the bar is higher, the problems are harder, and the feedback loops are faster. Anthropic is that place.

What I Bring

  • Production AI discipline — hybrid LLM + deterministic architecture, audit trails, dry-run defaults, cost governance, rollback plans. Every system built for operations teams, not demos.
  • Claude-native depth — I've built the ROSTR framework specifically for multi-agent Claude systems. PAL, NPAO, RAG/DAL, ContextEngine — published on Zenodo.
  • Enterprise GTM instincts — 15 years of sales at Uber, Upwork, Asana, Techstars. I find the real job behind the stated request and communicate across every level of the org.
  • Forward-deployed mindset — I build with adoption in mind. 50+ non-technical users relying on systems I built daily is the proof point.
  • Failure ownership — I own the runaway spend incident, the broken RAG architecture, the compliance bug caught on first live test. I investigate, fix, and document every time.
